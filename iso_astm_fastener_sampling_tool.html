Best Practice Guide

This guide helps users understand how to select a sampling approach. It is intentionally neutral because a manufacturer, distributor, and end user may reasonably evaluate the same lot differently.

ISO 2859-1: Attribute Acceptance Sampling

Use ISO 2859-1 concepts when the inspection is by attributes, such as pass/fail dimensional checks, visual defects, missing documentation, mixed parts, labeling errors, or go/no-go gaging. The plan is typically driven by lot size, inspection level, and AQL.

  • Lot size helps determine the sample size code letter.
  • Inspection level changes the sample size intensity.
  • AQL represents the quality level used to index the acceptance plan, not a guarantee that defects are acceptable.
  • Acceptance and rejection numbers must be verified against the controlled standard.

ASTM F1470: Fastener-Specific Sampling

Use ASTM F1470 guidance when sampling fasteners for specified mechanical properties, physical properties, performance properties, coating requirements, or other fastener quality requirements. Especially useful when the attribute is tied to fastener performance rather than simple visual sorting.

  • Commonly relevant for mechanical testing, coating requirements, physical properties, and performance requirements.
  • Dimensional and thread criteria may be governed by ASME B18, drawing requirements, customer specifications, or separate gaging plans.
  • Destructive or high-cost testing may use special inspection levels, engineering approval, or customer-defined sample sizes.

Attribute Severity

  • Critical: Safety, regulatory, severe functional risk, wrong material, wrong grade, hydrogen embrittlement concern, or failure mode with serious consequence.
  • Major: Fit, function, assembly, torque-tension behavior, coating performance, thread acceptance, or customer line disruption.
  • Minor: Low-risk variation unlikely to affect fit, function, safety, or customer use.
  • Cosmetic: Appearance-related concerns where function is not affected, unless the drawing or customer specification treats appearance as functional.

When to Escalate Beyond Normal Sampling

  • Safety-critical application or regulatory requirement.
  • Customer complaint, field issue, or repeated supplier rejection.
  • Mixed lot, uncertain traceability, missing certificates, or suspect material.
  • New supplier, new process, new coating source, or new part launch.
  • Characteristic cannot be reliably verified with a small sample.
  • Defect is not randomly distributed, such as plating rack marks, mixed parts, or process batch issues.

Recommended Workflow

  1. Confirm the controlled requirement: drawing, purchase order, customer specification, consensus standard, coating spec, or inspection instruction.
  2. Classify the characteristic: critical, major, minor, cosmetic, dimensional, mechanical, material, coating, thread/gaging, or documentation.
  3. Decide whether ISO 2859-1 or ASTM F1470 is the better guide for the characteristic.
  4. Select lot size range and inspection level.
  5. Use the recommended AQL as a starting point, then adjust for customer requirements and risk.
  6. Review risk flags. Escalate to tightened sampling, engineering review, supplier corrective action, or 100% inspection where appropriate.
  7. Record the final plan, actual sample size, acceptance criteria, inspector, date, and disposition.
