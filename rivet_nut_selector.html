Engineering Best Practice
Source: RivetKing catalog design considerations and AVK Engineering Manual EM-6. Always validate with prototype installations in your actual parent material before production release.

A. Grip Range — Actual vs. Theoretical

Always measure the actual assembled stack — not nominal sheet thicknesses. Burrs, formed edges, paint or coating buildup, and bends can change the effective grip significantly. Select a part code whose grip range comfortably brackets your measured stack.

B. Hole Size & Shape

Round bodies require round holes; hex and half-hex bodies require matching hex holes for anti-spin function. Calculate manufacturing tolerances plus paint/coating buildup to ensure the hole stays within specification.

C. Tool Clearance (Axial Access)

The installation tool must have straight axial access from above the hole. Select tooling during design — don't leave it to production. Poor alignment causes weak joints and rapid mandrel wear.

D. Head Clearance (Footprint)

Ensure the head flange can seat flat with no interference from brackets, bends, extrusions, or adjacent hardware. Lay out the head diameter plus tolerances on the drawing. This is the most common oversight in product design.

E. Blind-Side Clearance

The rivet nut collapses to form a backside flange. Ensure adequate clearance behind the sheet for the bulge to form and seat. Validate with trial installations — the collapsed height is shorter than the pre-installed height.

F. Parent Material Density

Plastics, composites, and soft aluminum require experimentation. For soft materials, quad-fold (IPB/IPN) or pre-bulbed (A-R) styles provide superior pull-out. Always perform trial installations in actual production material.

G. Mating Fastener Selection

Standard series (IKF, IKR, IRL, A-L, A-K) are designed for Grade 5 / Metric Class 8.8–9.8 mating screws. The A-T swaging series supports Grade 8 / Class 12.9. Never use prevailing-torque screws with round-body inserts.

H. Assembly Torque & Joint Design

The mating part must contact the insert head (not float over the hole). A gap between mating part and insert head allows the threaded nut portion to rotate or pull through. Mating part hole should be ~1.0 mm smaller than the insert head diameter.

I. Galvanic Corrosion

Avoid pairing dissimilar metals without isolation. An aluminum insert in a steel structure accelerates corrosion of the less-noble metal. For mixed-metal assemblies consider stainless steel inserts, isolating washers, or compatible platings.

J. Corrosion Protection & Plating

Standard zinc/yellow trivalent finishes provide ~120 hours SST to white rust. Enhanced finishes (Zinc-Nickel, Tin-Zinc trivalent) extend to 458–1000+ hours. Do not reprocess rivet nuts — plating thickness and lubricity affect installation torque and grip behavior.

K. Air Supply (Pneumatic Tools)

Pneumatic spin-spin tools (ARO) require 90–110 PSI dynamic air pressure at 25 SCFM. Use minimum 5/16″ ID hose and fittings, an inline oiler/separator, and a pressure regulator. Set pressure with the tool running, not static.

L. Production Release Checklist

Before releasing to production, document: parent material and grade, hole process (punch or drill), tool model and setting, pull-out test result, spin-out result, assembly torque window, and installation stroke/force.

Test Definitions

  • Pull-out: Force to extract insert; parent restrained by hold-down ring 3× body diameter
  • Push-out: Force to push insert through from back
  • Spin-out: Torque to rotate installed insert without influencing clamp load
  • Assembly torque: Suggested for Grade 5 / Class 8.8–9.8 mating hardware (dry)

Thread Class

Standard rivet nuts are manufactured to Unified 2B/2B per ASME B1.1 (inch) and 6H per ASME B1.13M (metric). Swaging series (A-T, ISR, ISN) are manufactured oversized to compensate for thread shrinkage during swaging.

Cautions

  • Do NOT reprocess rivet nuts — plating + wax affect installation
  • Test in actual application material
  • Cadmium plating is non-RoHS — confirm regulatory acceptability
  • PVC foam seals not recommended for petroleum-based liquids

Corrosion Upgrade Path

Standard → Zinc Clear (SZ/3T): 48–120 hrs SST
Upgrade → Yellow Zinc Trivalent (SY/4T): ~120 hrs
Enhanced → Zinc Nickel (SZN/10): 800+ hrs
Premium → Tin-Zinc Trivalent (9T): 458–800 hrs
Max → Stainless body: 1000+ hrs
