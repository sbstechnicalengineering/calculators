Why Consensus Standard Parts?

Using consensus standard fasteners over custom "manufactured to print" parts offers significant advantages across cost, quality, and supply chain flexibility.

💰 Cost Advantage

Standard parts are manufactured in high volumes by multiple suppliers worldwide, driving down unit cost through competition and economies of scale. Custom print parts carry tooling, setup, and minimum order premiums.

⏱ Lead Time

Standard fasteners are stocked globally. When you need 500 pieces of an M10 hex bolt, they ship today. A custom fastener may require 8–16 weeks for tooling, first article inspection, and production.

✅ Quality Assurance

Consensus standards (IFI, ISO, ASME, DIN, SAE) define dimensional tolerances, mechanical properties, and test methods. Independent certification and traceability are built in. No need to specify — the standard does it for you.

🔗 Supply Chain Flexibility

Standard parts can be sourced from dozens of manufacturers worldwide. If one supplier has a shortage or quality issue, you can qualify an alternate in days, not months.

📐 Engineering Confidence

Published standards provide documented design data: proof loads, tensile strength, torque values, and dimensional drawings. Your engineering team can rely on published data rather than generating it themselves.

♻️ Sustainability

Standardized designs reduce engineering waste and the need for custom tooling. Globally stocked parts reduce transportation distances when sourced regionally. Traceability requirements in many standards support responsible sourcing.
