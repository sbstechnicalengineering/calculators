ASME B94.6 Standard Pitch Reference
ASME B94.6 defines five standard pitches for inch-based knurling. Highlighted rows indicate the recommended pitch range for common screw machine diameters (0.25 in to 1.00 in).
Pitch Designation Teeth per Inch (TPI) Tooth Spacing (in) Tooth Depth (in) Typical Dia. Range (in) Application
Fine 64 0.01563 0.00781 0.125 to 0.375 Small parts, watchmaking, instruments
Medium Fine 96 0.01042 0.00521 0.250 to 0.500 General purpose, thin wall parts
Medium 128 0.00781 0.00391 0.375 to 0.750 Most common, fastener heads, knobs
Medium Coarse 160 0.00625 0.00313 0.500 to 1.000 Grips, press inserts, couplings
Coarse 192 0.00521 0.00260 0.750 and larger Large diameter, heavy grip, pipe
DIN 82 Metric Pitch Reference
DIN 82 is the German standard for knurling. Pitches are specified in mm. These are commonly used in European and metric applications.
Pitch (mm) Designation Tooth Depth (mm) Tooth Angle Typical Dia. Range (mm) Application
0.4 Fine 0.20 60 deg 3 to 10 Precision instruments, small parts
0.6 Medium 0.30 60 deg 8 to 25 General use, metric fasteners
0.8 Coarse 0.40 60 deg 16 to 45 Grips, handles, knobs
1.0 Extra Coarse 0.50 60 deg 30 and larger Heavy grip, large components

Pattern Selection Guide
Straight

Grooves run parallel to the part axis. Provides grip resistance to axial forces. Cannot transmit torque on its own without a mechanical stop.

Best for: Set screws, bushings, press-fit pins, decorative accents on turned parts.
Diagonal (Helical)

Grooves are cut at an angle (30 deg typical). Provides grip in both axial and rotational directions. Available as left-hand or right-hand helix.

Best for: Drive shafts, gear locking features, handles that require rotational grip.
Diamond (Cross)

Formed by crossing two diagonal patterns. Produces a diamond or pyramidal tooth. Maximum grip in all directions. Most common industrial knurl pattern.

Best for: Thumb screws, knobs, handles, medical devices, any high-grip application.
Convex

Rounded raised profile. Used when a knurled insert must expand into a plastic or soft material hole. The convex profile bites into surrounding material.

Best for: Knurled brass inserts for plastic, heat-set inserts, electronics enclosures.
Concave

Inward curved profile. Used in soft or ergonomic grip applications. The concave form fits the contour of a fingertip naturally.

Best for: Medical device grips, precision adjustment knobs, pen bodies.

Knurling Troubleshooting Guide
Double Tracking or Doubling Pattern

Causes

  • Blank diameter is not an exact multiple of pitch
  • Incorrect knurl wheel pitch selected
  • Workpiece and wheel pitches do not mesh

Solutions

  • Use this calculator to find the corrected blank diameter
  • Ensure number of teeth is a whole integer
  • Adjust blank by turning to nearest corrected diameter
Shallow or Incomplete Teeth

Causes

  • Insufficient infeed pressure on knurl wheel
  • Knurl wheel worn or clogged with chips
  • Wrong pitch for the material hardness
  • Blank diameter too large for selected pitch

Solutions

  • Increase radial pressure; use plunge method
  • Clean wheel with brass brush; replace if worn
  • Switch to a coarser pitch for hard materials
  • Use the blank diameter correction in this tool
Chatter or Vibration Marks

Causes

  • Spindle speed too high
  • Knurl holder not rigid enough
  • Long workpiece with insufficient support
  • Loose tailstock or chuck

Solutions

  • Reduce spindle speed by 30 to 50 percent
  • Use a rigid knurl holder; minimize overhang
  • Support with live center on long parts
  • Check and tighten all toolholding components
Torn or Ragged Teeth

Causes

  • Feed rate too fast
  • Knurl wheel too fine for material
  • Cutting fluid not applied or wrong type
  • Gummy material (aluminum, copper) without lubrication

Solutions

  • Reduce feed to 0.010 to 0.030 in per revolution
  • Use a coarser pitch wheel
  • Apply cutting oil continuously during knurling
  • For aluminum: use kerosene or light oil
General Best Practices: Always plunge knurl wheels in fully before engaging feed. Use a feed rate of 0.010 to 0.030 in per revolution. Apply cutting fluid. Make multiple passes if depth is insufficient after first pass. Never reverse the lathe direction with knurl wheels engaged.
