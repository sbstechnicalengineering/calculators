Best Practice Notes

Use the right level of precision. Parametric formulas are excellent for catalog pages, freight estimates, kit weights, quoting, and rough cost modeling. For final shipping labels, PPAP, inspection, or automated warehouse weight controls, validate with actual scale weights by part number and lot.
ConsiderationRecommendation
Material densityUse alloy-specific density when known. Stainless, brass, aluminum, and titanium can vary by grade.
Thread geometryUsing pitch diameter or thread-equivalent diameter is better than using the full major diameter for fully threaded parts.
CoatingsMost thin electroplated finishes add very little mass, but heavy coatings, hot dip galvanizing, nylon patches, or sealers can matter.
NutsSubtract the threaded hole. Apply a correction factor if chamfers, nylon collars, flange serrations, or distorted threads are significant.
WashersUse actual OD, ID, and thickness. Washer standards can vary significantly by series: narrow, regular, wide, hardened, structural, etc.
StandardsFor high confidence, load default dimensions from the controlling standard: ASME B18, ISO, DIN, IFI, ASTM, SAE, or customer print.
