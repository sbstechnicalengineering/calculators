Carbon Steel

What it is: The most widely used fastener material, composed primarily of iron and carbon (typically 0.05 to 0.95% carbon).
  • Low Carbon (1006 to 1022): Used for cold-formed parts like nuts and thin-wall locknuts. Very ductile, low strength.
  • Medium Carbon (1030 to 1045): Standard bolts and screws. Good balance of strength and machinability.
  • High Carbon (1045+): Higher strength applications, but reduced ductility.
Best for: General purpose, indoor, non-corrosive environments. Always specify a plating or coating for corrosion protection.

Alloy Steel (4140, 4037, 4137)

What it is: Carbon steel with added alloying elements (chromium, molybdenum, vanadium) that dramatically improve mechanical properties.
  • AISI 4140: The workhorse alloy for high-strength fasteners (B7 studs, Grade 8 bolts). Excellent hardenability.
  • Chromium-Moly-Vanadium (B16): Superior creep resistance at elevated temperatures. Used in pressure vessels and refineries.
Best for: High-strength structural, pressure vessel, and petrochemical applications. Requires coating for corrosion protection.

Austenitic Stainless Steel (300 Series)

What it is: Non-magnetic stainless steel with 18% chromium and 8 to 12% nickel. The most common stainless family.
  • 304 / 304L: Standard stainless. Good general corrosion resistance. 304L has lower carbon for weld applications.
  • 316 / 316L: Adds 2 to 3% molybdenum for superior resistance to chlorides and marine environments.
  • 347: Columbium-stabilized. Preferred for high-temperature service where sensitization is a concern.
Best for: Corrosive and food/chemical environments. Not recommended for high-strength structural use without strain hardening.

Ferritic Stainless Steel (400 Series)

What it is: Magnetic stainless steel with 10 to 18% chromium, no nickel. Less expensive than austenitic grades.
  • 410 (B6, B6X): Can be heat treated to achieve higher strength than 300 series. Good for moderate corrosion environments.
  • B5 (5 to 6% Cr-Mo): Designed for elevated temperature service in steam and petroleum equipment.
Best for: Moderately corrosive environments where higher strength is needed. Less corrosion resistant than 316 SS in chloride environments.

Aluminum Alloys

What it is: Lightweight fastener material approximately one-third the weight of steel.
  • 2024-T4: High strength aluminum used for aerospace applications.
  • 6061-T6: General purpose, good corrosion resistance and weldability.
  • 6262-T9: Improved machinability with bismuth and lead additions. Common for precision screws.
  • 7075-T73: Very high strength, excellent for aerospace. Good stress-corrosion cracking resistance.
Best for: Weight-sensitive applications, electronics, and aerospace. Not suitable for high-temperature or high-load structural use.

Weathering and Special Steels

What it is: Steels engineered for specific environmental or performance requirements.
  • Weathering Steel (A588): Forms a stable rust-like patina that protects the base metal. Used in bridges and outdoor structures.
  • Atmospheric Corrosion Resistant: Superior to plain carbon steel in outdoor environments without painting.
Best for: Exposed structural applications where painting is impractical. Not a substitute for stainless in highly corrosive or immersed environments.
