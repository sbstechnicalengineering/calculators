1.  VMI Services

VMI (Vendor-Managed Inventory) is a supply chain program in which Specialty Bolt & Screw manages your fastener and hardware inventory on-site at your facility. We monitor stock levels, replenish automatically on a defined schedule, and ensure you never run out of critical parts — reducing your purchasing workload, eliminating emergency buys, and lowering carrying costs.

SBS is a recognized expert in VMI solutions for OEM manufacturers across diverse industries and global locations. Whether you're in aerospace, automotive, medical, industrial manufacturing, or any other sector, we can design a customized VMI program for your operation. Learn more at specialtybolt.com.

VMI is ideal for OEM manufacturers, assemblers, and maintenance operations that use a consistent set of fasteners and hardware at predictable rates and want to eliminate the administrative burden of managing those items internally. It is particularly valuable for facilities with high part-number counts, lean/JIT production environments, or operations seeking to reduce inventory-related downtime.

Contact our team through the Contact Us page or call 800-322-7878 to schedule a consultation. We will assess your current inventory, usage patterns, and facility layout, and design a program that fits. Setup typically involves an on-site bin or Kanban replenishment system and an agreed restocking schedule.

3.  Materials, Grades & Coatings

Our Materials & Grades resource (Technical menu) provides a comprehensive reference covering common materials (alloy steel, stainless steel, aluminum, titanium, brass, etc.) and their associated strength grades and standards. Our Tech Dictionary is also a useful starting point for terminology.

Material type refers to the base alloy (e.g., 18-8 stainless steel, alloy steel, titanium Grade 5). Grade refers to the mechanical property classification within that material — for example, SAE Grade 5 vs. Grade 8 for carbon steel bolts, or A2-70 vs. A4-80 for stainless. Selecting the right combination depends on your strength, corrosion, and temperature requirements. Contact our Technical Support team if you need guidance.

We stock and source parts with a wide range of finishes including zinc, hot-dip galvanized, black oxide, passivated, nickel, chrome, Dacromet, and others. See our Plating & Coatings reference page for details. We can also apply alternative coatings to in-stock items as a secondary process — include your requirement in your RFQ or order notes.

Pre-applied patches are locking or sealing elements applied to the thread of a fastener before installation — they function as a controlled alternative to liquid threadlocking compounds. Common types include nylon pellets, full-thread nylon patches, and micro-encapsulated adhesive coatings. See our Pre-Applied Patches reference for details and options.

5.  Standards & Cross-Referencing

We stock and source parts conforming to a wide range of domestic and international standards including ASME/ANSI, ISO, DIN, JIS, SAE, IFI, MS (Military Standard), and NAS (National Aerospace Standard), among others. Our Standards Library provides access to consensus standards documentation to help you identify the correct specification for your application.

Use the Standards Library to browse and search available standards. If you know the part type but not the governing standard, our Request Technical Support form connects you with our engineering team, who can identify the applicable ASME, ISO, DIN, or other standard for your application.

These are different standards bodies that define specifications for fastener dimensions, tolerances, materials, and performance:

  • ASME/ANSI — American Society of Mechanical Engineers / American National Standards Institute (United States)
  • ISO — International Organization for Standardization (global, primarily metric)
  • DIN — Deutsches Institut für Normung (German origin, widely used in Europe)
  • JIS — Japanese Industrial Standards

Parts from different standards are often similar but may not be interchangeable due to subtle dimensional or thread differences. See also our Threads and Tech Dictionary references, or contact our technical team to confirm compatibility.

We carry parts in UNC, UNF, UNEF (unified inch series), metric coarse (M), metric fine (MF), and specialty thread forms. Our Threads & Thread Forms reference explains the differences and includes pitch charts and tolerance information. Our engineering team can also help confirm the correct thread form for your application.

6.  Custom & Special Parts / RFQ Process

Submit an RFQ when you need: a part not found in our online catalog; a standard part in a special material, finish, or dimension; a per-print custom part; large quantities that warrant a formal quote; or any item requiring specific documentation (MTR, C of C). Use our Request for Quote page to get started.

The more detail you provide, the faster and more accurately we can respond. Ideally include:

  • Part description or name
  • Governing standard (if known), e.g., ASME B18.3 or DIN 912
  • Material and grade (e.g., 316 SS, Grade 8, A286, Ti-6Al-4V)
  • Finish or plating (e.g., zinc yellow, passivated, black oxide)
  • Dimensions: thread size, length, head style, drive type, tolerances
  • Quantity required and whether blanket/scheduled releases are needed
  • Required documentation: MTR, C of C, RoHS, etc.
  • Required delivery date or lead time constraint

A print or drawing (PDF) can be attached and is especially helpful for per-print or screw machine parts.

SBS specializes in a broad range of custom and engineered fasteners and components, including:

  • Custom fasteners and per-print specials
  • CNC turned and screw machine parts
  • Stampings (progressive, deep-drawn, fine-blanking)
  • Brass inserts (pressed-in or molded-in)
  • Bushings, spacers, and plain bearings
  • Electrical standoffs and PCB hardware
  • Vacuum hardware (vented components for in-vacuum environments)
  • Titanium fasteners and components
  • Rivets, SEMS screws, cable ties, and clamps

See the RFQs and Sourcing menu for the full list of specialty categories.
