The information below covers key engineering considerations for selecting the correct flat washer. Always verify against applicable standards and consult a qualified engineer for safety-critical applications.
Washer Sizing
Inner Diameter (ID) Selection

The ID should be slightly larger than the bolt shank to allow free assembly. Most standards specify an ID clearance of approximately 5 to 15% over the nominal bolt diameter. Oversized IDs reduce bearing area and can lead to pull-through under load.

Outer Diameter (OD) Guidelines

Select OD based on the bearing area needed to distribute the clamp load without yielding the mating surface. For soft materials (aluminum, plastics, wood), use large-series washers (ISO 7093, fender) to spread load. For steel-to-steel joints with hardened surfaces, standard OD is generally sufficient.

Hardness Selection
Why Hardness Matters

Washer hardness must be matched to the bolt grade and joint requirements. A washer that is too soft will embed under load (reducing clamp force). A washer that is too hard may damage coating or plating on mating surfaces.

Key Rules
  • Never use unhardened washers (F844, ISO 7091) under high-strength bolts (Grade 10.9 / Grade 8 and above)
  • ASTM F436 hardened washers are required under the turned element (nut or bolt head) when tightening ASTM A325 and A490 structural bolts
  • For stainless steel bolts, match washer hardness to avoid galling

Galvanic Corrosion Warning

Mixing dissimilar metals accelerates corrosion. Match washer material to bolt material where possible. When mixing is unavoidable, use an insulating washer or dielectric coating between dissimilar metals (e.g., steel bolts in aluminum structure).

Decision Checklist
  • Is the joint structural or safety-critical? Use F436 or ISO 7089 / 7090 at minimum
  • Is the substrate soft (aluminum, plastic, wood)? Use large series (ISO 7093)
  • Is space constrained under a cheese head? Use ISO 7415
  • Is the environment corrosive? Select stainless or coated washers
  • Does the standard need to match a bolt spec? Confirm HV rating compatibility
Installation and Inspection
Correct Orientation
  • Chamfered washers (ISO 7090): chamfer faces toward the bolt head or nut (turned element)
  • Place the smooth face against the mating surface to avoid damage to coatings
  • Do not stack multiple washers as a substitute for correct thickness selection
Pre-Installation Checks
  • Verify ID clears the bolt shank and thread runout
  • Confirm OD does not overlap adjacent fasteners or features
  • Check washer is flat and free of burrs or rolled edges that could affect seating
  • For structural joints, confirm F436 is required and verify heat number traceability if applicable
Reuse Policy

Hardened structural washers (F436) should generally not be reused after a full pretension cycle. Plain washers in non-structural applications may be reused if undamaged. Inspect for embedding, cracking, or deformation before reuse.

Tolerance and Fit
Product Grade A vs C

Grade A (ISO 7089, 7090, 7093) specifies tighter tolerances on ID, OD, and flatness, making it suitable for precision and structural applications. Grade C (ISO 7091) has wider tolerances and is used in lower-precision or non-critical general assembly.

Tolerance Interaction with Bolt Clearance
  • The nominal ID in most standards already includes clearance over the bolt shank
  • The upper ID tolerance adds further clearance, which is acceptable for load bearing
  • The lower ID tolerance (typically 0) ensures the washer always clears the bolt shank without forcing
  • For high-vibration environments, smaller ID clearance helps maintain washer position
Thickness Tolerance Notes

Thickness tolerance affects joint preload consistency. Thicker-than-nominal washers can reduce effective bolt engagement. For precision joints, verify against the tolerance band in the applicable standard document.
