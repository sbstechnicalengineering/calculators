Important Disclaimer
This calculator is a guide to estimate torque values. Use of its content by anyone is the sole responsibility of that person and they assume all risk. Due to many variables that affect the torque-tension relationship — including human error, surface texture, and lubrication — the only way to determine the correct torque is through experimentation under actual joint and assembly conditions.
When tightening is critical to the application: SELECT TORQUE BY EXPERIMENTATION
Recommended procedure: torque 10+ parts to failure in the actual application. Average the failure torque. Seating torque should be 65%–75% of average failure torque.
Calculation Method
FormulaT = KDP — where T = Torque, K = Torque Coefficient (dimensionless), D = Nominal Diameter, P = Bolt Clamp Load
Clamp Load Basis83% of minimum proof load for each grade and size (adjustable via slider)
K — Waxed (pressure wax)0.10
K — Hot Dip Galvanized0.25
K — Plain / As Received0.20
Tension VariabilityBolt tension varies ±25% when tightened with an indicating torque wrench
