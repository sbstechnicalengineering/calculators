      Best Practice Guide: Passivation vs Electropolishing
      Core Difference
      Passivation
      • Removes free iron and surface contaminants
      • Enhances the natural chromium oxide passive layer
      • Does NOT remove base material
      • No dimensional change to the part
      • Chemical treatment only (no current applied)
      Electropolishing
      • Electrochemically removes a thin layer of metal
      • Exposes clean, contaminant-free base material
      • Smooths surface peaks and micro-asperities
      • Removes embedded particles from machining or handling
      • Requires electrical current and electrolyte bath
      Corrosion Resistance
      Passivation
      • Removes free iron that can initiate rust
      • Restores or improves the natural oxide layer
      • Effective when base material is already clean and sound
      Electropolishing
      • Removes embedded contaminants at a deeper level
      • Creates a more uniform and chromium-enriched passive layer
      • Demonstrated 30x or more improvement in salt spray testing vs passivation alone
      For marine, chemical process, and high-humidity environments, electropolishing is strongly preferred when surface condition permits.
      Surface Finish Impact
      Passivation
      • No change to surface roughness (Ra)
      • Does not improve cosmetic appearance
      • Will not remove machining marks, burrs, or discoloration
      Electropolishing
      • Reduces Ra by approximately 50% by removing surface peaks
      • Produces a bright, reflective finish
      • Removes discoloration from heat tint (with pre-treatment)
      Cleanliness and Particle Removal
      Passivation
      • Removes loose surface contamination
      • Cannot remove embedded machining particles
      • Adequate for standard industrial cleanliness
      Electropolishing
      • Removes embedded iron, carbide particles, and inclusions
      • Required for semiconductor-grade and high-purity wetted surfaces
      • Preferred for implantable medical devices and FDA-regulated applications
      Deburring
      Passivation
      • Does not remove burrs or sharp edges
      • Parts must be deburred prior to treatment
      Electropolishing
      • Removes micro-burrs and sharp edges
      • No mechanical stress introduced during deburring
      • Ideal for precision parts where mechanical deburring is not acceptable
      Geometry and Process Risk
      Passivation
      • Safe for blind holes, threads, and complex recesses
      • Acid rinses freely from all geometry
      • Preferred for assemblies that cannot be fully immersed or rinsed
      Electropolishing
      • Electrolyte can be trapped in blind holes if not properly rinsed
      • Current density is lower in recessed areas, reducing effectiveness
      • Parts must be oriented and fixtured to ensure complete electrolyte drainage
      For parts with deep blind holes or complex internal geometry, consult the processor before specifying electropolishing.
      Dimensional Impact
      Passivation
      • No material removal, no dimensional change
      • Safe for tight-tolerance parts at final dimension
      Electropolishing
      • Removes 0.0002 in to 0.001 in of material (typical)
      • Dimensional allowance must be built into the machining drawing
      • Thread fit should be verified post-process for critical assemblies
      Applicable Standards
      ASTM A967 - Passivation AMS 2700 - Passivation ASTM B912 - Electropolishing
      Always confirm the applicable revision of the standard with your processor. Print callouts should reference the standard and method (e.g., ASTM A967 Method C - Nitric 2, or ASTM B912).
      Key Decision Summary
      Choose Passivation When
      • Low-to-moderate corrosion environment
      • Tight tolerances and no material removal allowed
      • Complex geometry with blind holes or threads
      • Cost-sensitive application
      • Print specifies ASTM A967 or AMS 2700
      Choose Electropolishing When
      • Maximum corrosion resistance required
      • Smooth, low Ra, or cosmetic finish required
      • Semiconductor, medical, or high-purity application
      • Embedded particle removal is critical
      • Micro-deburring is needed and mechanical deburring is not acceptable
